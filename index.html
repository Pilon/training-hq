Guide

The 100k rules

Fueling — the race-decider
  • 40–60 g carbs/hour from hour one, a feed every ~40 min, 500–750 ml fluid/hour (more in heat).
  • First feed at ~45 min, before you feel empty. Nothing new in the final 30 min.
  • Practice the exact products and timing on every Phase 2+ long ride — the rehearsal locks the menu, then nothing new on race day.
  • Carry ~4 feeds + 2 bottles (water + electrolyte/carb mix); practice grabbing refills at aid stations.
The escarpment (km 0–9)
  • ~140 m of sustained climbing right at the start — the race’s trap.
  • Low gear, seated, 80+ rpm, Z3 ceiling. Matches burned here cost triple over the next 90 km.
  • Train for it: hill repeats and 15-min KICKR climb sims are in the plan (that’s the escarpment’s duration).
Descending (the route ends downhill)
  • The finish descends the escarpment (~260 → 96 m). Practice on the Sep 12 rehearsal.
  • Hands in the drops, weight back, brake before corners — never in them. Calm beats fast.
Comfort = speed at 4+ hours
  • Padded bibs + chamois cream on every 60 km+ ride. Any numbness = immediate fit follow-up.
  • Stand 10–15 s every few km on long rides; move hands between tops/hoods/drops.
  • Post-Stratford fit follow-up is part of the plan — tell the fitter about the 4–5 h target.
Pacing the day
  • After the climb: Z2–low Z3 all day. If in doubt, slower early — a strong final 25 km is the win condition.
  • Effort steady, speed variable: gear down early on rises, recover on descents.
  • The Sep 12 rehearsal sets the realistic finish target (~4:00–4:30 expected on current trajectory).
